Recommended Diet Plan


  • CALISTHENICS

    Just like calisthenics exercises, a calisthenics diet is actually very simple and straightforward. You don’t really need a cheat sheet to know if you are eating the right stuff or if you are eating the correct weight of food. It is very flexible and can be adjusted depending on your fitness goals.
    Following diet plan is highly recommended :-
    Focus on whole foods like lean protein, fresh produce like fruits and vegetables, grains and dairy.
    Proteins include meat, fish, seafood, and eggs.
    Eliminate junk food, processed food and empty carbs. This means giving up sweets, cakes, pastries, chips, soft drinks, juices high in sugar, processed foodstuff, sugary cereals, cookies, white rice and pasta, white bread and many more similar products.
    Shorten your eating window. Many calisthenics athletes practice Intermittent Fasting or IF. This is when you limit the time you can consume food to only 8 hours in a day (except water, tea or coffee).
    Multivitamins – A good vitamin supplement should include Vitamin C, D, E, B complex, Zinc and Magnesium among others.

    Do all the exercises in the order shown, resting 30 seconds between exercises and 3 minutes afterward. Repeat for 3 rounds.
    10 pullups
    10 chinups
    20 clips
    25 jump squats
    20 pushups
    50 crunches
    10 burpees
    30 seconds jumping rope

    ->The circuit training does also provide cardiovascular endurance because of the short rest periods. And with the metabolic gains from the resistance training, effects could last up to 72 hours due to the level of intensity the workout provides. Hence, this should only be trained around 2-3 times per week to maximize rest.
    ->This full body circuit training will do just that, excellent for intermediate level athletes. Get rid of your stubborn fat to look more ripped and leaner than ever by combining aerobic and resistance training in a single workout.
    ->But the benefits don’t stop there! Since you’re going to train with high volume with enough intensity, you could still build strength and develop muscle mass with the routine. This holds true if the amount of reps leads you to near volitional failure.

    Bench Dips - Triceps, lats, and chest are your main muscles for the exercise, but mostly, you’ll feel the burn on your triceps. When performing the exercise, Do not let your elbows flare outwards. Keep them near your body and parallel to each other.

    Pull Ups - Your biceps and back workout monster. Keep the form in check in each repetition. Do not force your neck over the bar just for the sake of “getting over the bar”. We don’t want any loose momentum.

    Squats - Train your quads, hamstrings, and glutes with squats. Just remember the key points when performing the movement. Do not let your knees move past your toes. Control the concentric and eccentric movement.

    Calf Raises - Calf muscles are an important muscle group when generating power from your legs so have to train them with raises. When performing the exercise, do each leg one at a time to correct possible muscle imbalances and practice your balance.

    Close Grip chinups - Finish the workout round with a biceps building exercise. The pointers you have to keep in mind for this exercise is basically the same as the pull-ups; but instead of driving your elbows to your waist
